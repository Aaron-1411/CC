# transparenC — Mission

## Why this exists

UK democracy has an information problem.

The data that should hold government to account — spending records, party promises, voting behaviour, lobbying registers, NHS performance, water company discharge hours — is technically public. It lives in spreadsheets on GOV.UK, API endpoints nobody knows about, and PDFs buried three clicks deep on departmental websites.

Politicians rely on that friction. Short public memory and inaccessible information are features of the current system, not bugs. The gap between what government says and what it does is rarely exposed because the evidence is too scattered to act on.

transparenC closes that gap.

---

## What we do

We pull live data from the authoritative public sources — Parliament, the Electoral Commission, NHS England, the Environment Agency, the ONS, Contracts Finder, IPSA, data.police.uk, DWP, and more — and surface it in one place, in plain English, free of editorial spin.

Every number links to its original source. Every party pledge shows its delivery status with evidence. Every government contract is searchable. Every MP's voting record is readable by a teenager.

We don't interpret the data for you. We just make sure you can find it.

---

## Core principles

**Non-partisan.** We apply the same scrutiny to every party, every government, every era. If a pledge is broken, it's broken regardless of who made it. If performance has improved, it's improved regardless of who is in power.

**Primary sources only.** No think-tank summaries, no newspaper interpretations. The data comes from the bodies that collect it and is linked back to them directly.

**Plain English.** Government data is often designed to obscure. We translate it — without losing accuracy.

**Open access.** Free. No account required. No paywall. Public data should be publicly usable.

**Built on open standards.** All source material is under the Open Government Licence v3.0 or equivalent. Our tools exist to make that licence mean something in practice.

---

## Who this is for

Everyone who pays taxes, uses public services, or votes — which is to say, everyone.

But especially:

- People who want to know whether politicians kept their promises
- Journalists and researchers who need a fast starting point for investigations
- Young people engaging with politics for the first time
- Anyone who's been told something is happening and wants to check
- Local campaigners and community organisers who need evidence behind their arguments
- People who've given up on politics because it all feels like noise

---

## What we are not

We are not a news outlet. We do not publish opinion or analysis.

We are not aligned with any political party, campaign, or movement.

We are not a replacement for journalism, advocacy or democratic participation — we are infrastructure that makes those things easier and better-informed.

---

## The long-term aim

A country where it is harder for politicians to make promises they don't intend to keep — because anyone can check, with two clicks, whether they did.

Where "the government says X" is a statement that can be verified or refuted in seconds, not hours.

Where civic participation — from a first-time voter to a veteran campaigner — is grounded in fact rather than impression.

That's the version of democracy this project is working towards. One data point, one broken promise, one searchable contract at a time.
